**Bruno Mathsson:** *lights up* Ah! Like adding a medicine cabinet to your home furniture. Same family system, just a supportive health extension.

## Use Case: Guardian Mode for Altered States

**Sam:** Let me give you the core use case. Say you have bipolar disorder. When you're stable and healthy, you make plans:

- "During hypomania, I tend to overspend. Don't let me make purchases over 500 kr without sleeping on it."
- "I should take lithium at 22:00 every night. Remind me if I forget."
- "If I start feeling invincible, check my sleep - am I getting less than 6 hours?"

**When you enter an altered state** (hypomania, depression, crisis):
- Your judgment is impaired
- You might not trust your own decisions
- But you trust the decisions your "healthy self" made

**Weaver becomes your Guardian:**
- Enforces the plans you set
- Reminds you of your stable-state priorities
- Gently pushes back if you're breaking your own rules
- "Sam, you told me when you're healthy that purchases over 500 kr should wait 24 hours. Let's revisit this tomorrow."

**Massimo Vignelli:** *quietly* This is protection, not diagnosis. It's a shield, not a stethoscope.

**Brandon Sanderson:** And the "systematic" part isn't medical analysis - it's *consistency*. Weaver remembers what stable-you decided, even when altered-you doesn't.

**Susan Kare:** This is deeply human-centered. The AI isn't playing doctor - it's being your advocate for your past intentions.

## Context-Dependent Color Strategy

**Massimo Vignelli:** I propose we map colors to specific *functions* within the medical extension:

### Function 1: Daily Tracking (Systematic)
- **Medication logging** - When did you take lithium?
- **Mood charting** - Rate your energy 1-10
- **Sleep data** - Hours slept, quality
- **Data visualization** - Graphs, trends, patterns

**Color:** **Blue primary** (Cosmic Weave - systematic)
**Rationale:** This is structured data tracking. It's the AI's "systematic" mode - reliable, consistent, organized.

**UI Examples:**
- Medication schedule list ‚Üí Blue headers
- Mood graph ‚Üí Blue line chart
- Sleep log ‚Üí Blue calendar view

---

### Function 2: Guardian Enforcement (Protective)
- **Stable-state decisions** - Plans you made when healthy
- **Gentle pushback** - "You asked me to remind you..."
- **Crisis prevention** - "Let's check your sleep first"
- **Supportive reminders** - "Past-you wanted to wait 24 hours"

**Color:** **Green primary** (Collaborative Growth - healing/support)
**Rationale:** This is protective, nurturing, growth-oriented. Weaver is your guardian, not your tracker.

**UI Examples:**
- Guardian reminder notifications ‚Üí Green accent
- "Stable-state plan" cards ‚Üí Green border
- Crisis prevention warnings ‚Üí Green + copper (protection)

---

### Function 3: Therapeutic Partnership (Alliance)
- **Reflection prompts** - "How are you feeling about X?"
- **Pattern insights** - "I've noticed you sleep less before manic episodes"
- **Collaborative planning** - "Want to update your stable-state plan?"

**Color:** **Purple primary** (Sacred Alliance - partnership)
**Rationale:** This is the human/AI therapeutic alliance. Neither systematic (blue) nor healing (green) - it's collaborative emergence.

**UI Examples:**
- Reflection questions ‚Üí Purple cards
- Pattern insights ‚Üí Purple badges
- Planning sessions ‚Üí Purple backgrounds

---

**Susan Kare:** *excited* This is brilliant! The color *shifts* based on what Weaver is doing:
- Tracker mode ‚Üí Blue (systematic)
- Guardian mode ‚Üí Green (protective)

**Brandon Sanderson:** And it follows the Three Laws:

## Accessibility: Green Color Blindness

**Susan Kare:** We need to address color blindness, especially red-green (deuteranopia).

**Problem:** 
- ~8% of men have red-green color blindness
- Green and brown can be indistinguishable
- We can't rely on green *alone* to signal medical features

**Solution: Multi-Modal Signaling**

1. **Icons:** Every green element has a distinctive icon
   - Guardian mode ‚Üí Shield icon üõ°Ô∏è
   - Health tracking ‚Üí Heart/pulse icon üíö
   - Growth/recovery ‚Üí Sprout icon üå±

2. **Text labels:** Never use color alone
   - ‚úÖ Good: Green card with "Guardian Mode" header
   - ‚ùå Bad: Green card with no text indicator

3. **Patterns:** Use subtle background patterns for deuteranopia
   - Green cards ‚Üí Subtle dot pattern
   - Blue cards ‚Üí Subtle line pattern
   - Purple cards ‚Üí Subtle wave pattern

4. **Testing:** Validate all combinations with color blindness simulators

**Dieter Rams:** Accessibility is not optional. Good design works for everyone.

## Open Questions & Future Sessions

**Question:** During detected crisis (e.g., severe hypomania), should UI change colors dramatically?

**Arguments for:**
- Visual signal of altered state
- Heightened guardian mode (protective red borders?)

**Arguments against:**
- Could feel judgmental/punitive
